AE 2010 Project

Partner: Andrew Ma

Professor: Jerry Seitzman (AE 2010)

We designed a double supersonic converging-diverging nozzle to test a specific wedge shape. We used MATLAB extensively to calculate dimensions, startup power, and various thermodynamic properties based on our design space (working fluid, test section Mach number, and inlet stagnation temperature) then graphed the results in 2D and 3D plots. We then related our findings to the costs required to run the wind tunnel.

ME 1670 Group Project

Partners: Marc Nassar, Jake Boudreau, Alex Hammonds, Aaryak Shastri

Professor: Raghuram Pucha

Using SOLIDWORKS, we designed a multipurpose kitchen tool that utilized a central power system to double as a blender and kettle. I worked on the blender attachment, ensuring it could screw onto the rest of the assembly. This project required an extreme commitment to teamwork and collaboration, as each of our individual designs would eventually combine into an assembly. At the end, we created a SOLIDWORKS animation that demonstrated the way the assembly would integrate each separate attachment.

ME 1670 Individual Project

Professor: Raghuram Pucha

This project took me through the entire creative process, from ideation to sketching to CADing to printing. The design was an efficient storage unit for oral hygiene products, which I eventually 3D printed and used as intended.

AE 1601 Glider Project

Professor: Kelly Griendling

Armed with a basic knowledge of aerodynamics after nearing the end of the introductory AE class, we were tasked with making a hand thrown glider using readily available materials. My project video, which ended up being the deliverable for the project along with a in person flight test competition, details the design choices I implemented to create a unique and effective glider.
